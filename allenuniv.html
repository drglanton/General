Heritage & Legacy

History & Identity

Allen University was founded in 1870 as Payne Institute by ministers of the African Methodist Episcopal (AME) Church, with the original mission of educating freed slaves and newly emancipated African Americans. In 1880, it relocated to Columbia, SC and was renamed Allen University in honor of Bishop Richard Allen — the founder of the AME Church — making it the first university in South Carolina founded by African Americans for African Americans.

The campus itself is listed on the National Register of Historic Places as the Allen University Historic District. Chappelle Auditorium is historically significant — it was the site of the original meeting that set in motion Brown v. Board of Education.

Today Allen is a small, faith-grounded liberal arts institution with an estimated $30M+ annual economic impact on the Columbia community, and a strong legacy of producing educators, clergymen, and elected officials.

Allen is one of the most affordable HBCUs in the country. At roughly $13K/year in tuition before aid, an athletic scholarship can dramatically reduce your total cost. The average student receiving grant aid pays approximately $13,862 net price — and athletic aid stacks on top of academic aid in many cases. Come prepared to discuss the full financial picture.

Yellow Jackets Basketball

The Program

Head Coach: Doug Whittler

Coach Whittler was hired in June 2025, arriving from Morehouse College where he spent four years as head coach and compiled a 76-40 overall record (65.5% win rate) and 59-21 in SIAC conference play (73.4%) — the highest win total for any Morehouse coach in their first four seasons.

His Morehouse teams won three consecutive SIAC division titles. He was also selected as one of four head coaches nationally to serve as co-head coach in the 2025 HBCU All-Star Game.

He was named interim head coach at Morehouse in 2021 following the passing of longtime coach Grady Brewer, then elevated to full head coach and immediately produced winning seasons.

Bottom line: Coach Whittler is a proven, high-level SIAC coach who came to Allen to build something. This is a ground-floor opportunity at a program on the rise.

The 2025–26 season is Coach Whittler's first at Allen. The team was predicted to finish last in the SIAC in the preseason — a motivating chip on the shoulder. Early in the season they were competitive in close games, showing strong character despite tight losses.

A December 2025 game recap described the team as "on the cusp of turning the corner — competing toe-to-toe with SIAC opponents and proving they belong in every game." Key returning player Jacquez Shuler (junior forward) logged a double-double (13 pts, 10 reb, 5 ast) in a competitive loss.

This is a rebuilding year with a high-caliber new coaching staff — exactly the kind of program where a skilled recruit can come in and earn immediate playing time and impact.

Be Prepared. Be Sharp.

Questions to Ask the Coach

Asking the right questions shows maturity, preparation, and genuine interest. Don't just sit back — coaches are evaluating how you think and carry yourself off the court as much as on it. Here are the most important questions, grouped by category.

🏀 About the Program & Your Role

01

What does my role look like on this team — and what do I need to do to earn more responsibility?

Shows you're competitive and coachable. Tells you if they see you as a starter, rotational player, or project.

02

What does your system look like — and where do you see my skill set fitting in?

Understand if their style of play suits your game (pace, spacing, defensive scheme, positional roles).

03

How many scholarships are available, and what does a scholarship package look like here?

NCAA D-II programs have a maximum of 10 men's basketball scholarships. Know exactly what's on the table — full vs. partial.

04

Where do you see this program in two to three years, and what's your vision for building it?

Given Coach Whittler's track record at Morehouse, you want to hear his plan. Shows you think long-term.

05

What separates the players who thrive here from the ones who don't?

Lets you assess cultural fit and understand what the coach values most in his players.

💰 About Scholarship & Finances

06

Can athletic aid be combined with academic scholarships or financial aid to cover the full cost of attendance?

Critical financial question. Some schools stack aid, others don't. Allen's low tuition means a partial scholarship can go very far.

07

What happens to my scholarship if I get injured? Is it renewable each year?

Protects your interests. You want guaranteed multi-year aid, not a single-year offer that can be pulled.

08

Is there a NIL program or collective here, and how does that work for athletes?

Allen has a Yellow Jacket NIL Collective — real supplemental income opportunity worth asking about.

📚 About Academic Support

09

What academic resources are available specifically for student-athletes — tutoring, study hall, advisors?

Shows you take academics seriously. Also important info — you need to know you'll be supported through the season grind.

10

How does the coaching staff handle it when academic schedules conflict with practice or travel?

Real-world question every student-athlete faces. Good coaches have a clear answer.

11

Are players able to do internships or pursue career development while playing?

Especially relevant if you're interested in Sports Management or Business — the degree needs to work for your future.

🏠 About Campus Life

12

Where do most athletes live — on campus or off? And what is housing like for freshmen?

Allen has residence halls (Williams Residence Halls). Know what you're walking into logistically.

13

What's the culture like on the team — how do the players bond and support each other?

Culture eats talent. You want teammates who push each other, not just tolerate each other.

14

Can I speak with a current player today about their experience here?

The best intel comes from a current player speaking candidly — not the coach's sales pitch.

🔮 About Next Steps

15

What does your recruiting timeline look like, and when do you need a decision?

Understand the urgency. NCAA D-II scholarship offers can have deadlines. Don't let the offer expire by waiting too long.

16

What do you need to see from me — in the workout today and going forward — before you can make a formal offer?

Directly asks the coach to show his cards. If he's serious, he'll tell you exactly what the threshold is.

Today's Workout

How to Prepare & Perform

Before You Step on the Court

Hydrate on the drive down

Start drinking water now — not when you arrive. You want to be fully hydrated, not gulping water 20 minutes before tip.

Eat a light, quality meal 2–3 hours before

Avoid heavy or greasy food. Lean protein, complex carbs (rice, sweet potato, oatmeal), light on sugar.

Have your gear ready and organized

Clean basketball shoes (non-marking), shorts, practice jersey, socks, change of clothes, towel. Don't scramble when you arrive.

Arrive 15–20 minutes early

Introduce yourself to coaches calmly. Look around. Shake hands with players. This is part of the evaluation before the ball drops.

Warm up your body on your own time

Light jog, dynamic stretches, ball handling. Don't go into drills cold — do this before they officially start.

During the Workout — Mindset

✅ DO

  • Compete hard on every rep, drill, and possession — even the boring ones
  • Talk on defense. Coaches love communicators.
  • Show your real skill set — don't try to be someone else
  • Sprint back on every play, especially when you make a mistake
  • Be coachable — respond immediately when a coach corrects you
  • Play with energy and joy — enjoy yourself
  • Introduce yourself to every player and coach by name

🚫 DON'T

  • Ball hog or show off — play within the offense
  • Jog back on defense or take any play off
  • React negatively to being guarded hard or physical play
  • Be on your phone between drills
  • Argue calls or show frustration at teammates
  • Try to do too much — coaches want basketball IQ, not highlight reels

🎯 What Coaches Are Actually Watching

  • Effort & motor: Do you compete every single possession, drill, or sprint? This is the #1 thing evaluated in a workout. You cannot control your talent level that day; you can always control your effort.
  • Coachability: When a coach gives you a correction, do you immediately adjust or push back? They want to coach you — show them you're teachable.
  • Character off the ball: What do you do when you don't have the ball? Screens, cuts, positioning — these separate skilled players from team players.
  • Body language: After a mistake, do you shake it off and get back into position or sulk? Mental toughness is evaluated every minute.
  • Basketball IQ: Do you make the right pass? Do you understand spacing? Can you read the defense? Coach Whittler won 65% of his games at Morehouse — he coaches high-IQ basketball.
